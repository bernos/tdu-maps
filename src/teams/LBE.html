LOTTO - BELISOL

THE RIDERS: DID YOU KNOW?

Six of the seven riders, listed as part of the Lotto-Belsiol team line up for the 2012 Santos Tour Down Under rode the 2011 event, as the Omega Pharma-Lotto team.

New Zealand's Greg Henderson joins the Lotto-Belisol team for the 2012 season from Sky Pro Cycling, who he rode for in 2011 and at last year's race.

Andre Greipel returns to ride the Santos Tour Down Under in 2012. In 2010 Greipel became only the second person in the event's history to win two editions of the race. He won in 2008 and 2010.

At the 2011 Santos Tour Down Under Greipel finished seventh overall in the General Classification and sixth overall in the Jayco Sprint Classification.

In 2011, Andre Greipel won his first stage at the Tour de France on Stage 10, beating his biggest rival and former team mate Mark Cavendish.

Jürgen Roelandts won the Cycle Instead Young Rider Jersey at the 2010 Santos Tour Down Under.

In 2009, Adam Hansen proved himself as the ultimate teammate, helping the team to many victories including those at the Tour of California, Dauphiné Libéré, and the Vuelta a Espana.

In 2010 Adam was a stage winner and yellow jersey wearer at Ster Elektrotoer.

Olivier Kaisen won stage 5 of the Tour of Turkey in 2009.

Marcel Sieberg took fifth place in the Omloop Het Nieuwsblad in 2010.

Vicente Reynes previously rode for HTC-Highroad with Andre Greipel and moved with Andrew to the then named Omega Pharma-Lotto team.

THE TEAM: DID YOU KNOW?

Belisol is a renowned Belgian company that also has ambitions internationally, and has experience in sports. Belisol announced a three year partnership with the team and is an ideal partner for the team's historic sponsor Lotto.

Belgium Lotto has a long history of cycling sponsorship, commencing in 1984 with Tönissteiner-Lotto-Mavic-Pecotex. In 1985, it became prime sponsor of a team bearing only its name.
