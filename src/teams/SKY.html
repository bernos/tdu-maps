SKY PROCYCLING

THE RIDERS: DID YOU KNOW?

Geraint Thomas, Christopher Sutton, Michael Rogers and Mathew Hayman all rode the 2011 Santos Tour Down Under.

Michael Rogers is an overall winner of the Santos Tour Down Under, taking this honour in 2002.

Edvald Boasson Hagen is the current Norwegian National Time Trial Champion for the fifth year running (2007, 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011).

Edvald is a two time Tour de France stage winner (2011 Tour de France, Stage 6 and Stage 17). His stage win on Stage 6 made it the first ever victory by a British registered professional cycling team in the Tour de France.

In August, he took a clean sweep of the jerseys at the Eneco Tour, winning the event overall, as well as the points and young riders competitions, and finishing first on the final stage.

Thomas started the year with some promising performances, finishing sixth in the Classica Sarda and second overall in the Dwars door Vlaanderen. He claimed his first professional victory in May 2011, winning the five day Bayern-Rundfahrt.

At the 2011 Tour de France, Thomas finished sixth on the opening stage, to take the White Young Riders Jersey which he retained from stages 1 - 7. He finished third on Stage 2 and fifth on Stage 5.

Geraint finished 31st overall in the 2011Tour de France. He was also part of the Great Britain team t the 2011 UCI Road race Championships, assisting to lead out Mark Cavendish to victory.

In 2010 Thomas won the British National Road Race Champion title.

Chris Sutton had a great 2011 season, finishing first on Stage 2, Vuelta a Espana, first in the Kuurne-Brussel-Kuurne, second on Stage 1 Jayco Bay Classic and fourth on Stage 1 Santos Tour Down Under.

Christopher Sutton claimed victory on Stage 6 of the 2010 Santos Tour Down Under and finished 39th overall.

Alex Dowsett joined Sky in 2011 and had an impressive season.

He is the current British National Time Trail Champion (2011), claimed stage honours on 8a Tour of Britain, first in the London Nocturne and second overall in the Tour du Poitou-Charentes with an overall stage win in stage 5 of this race.

Danny Pate will join the Sky Pro Cycling team for the 2012 season.

In 2011, Pate was first overall in the King of the Mountains Competition at the Grand Prix Cycliste de Montreal.

Mathew Hayman had an impressive 2011 season, finishing first in the Paris-Bourges race and tenth overall in the Paris - Roubaix.

Mathew Hayman won a gold medal at the 2006 Melbourne Commonwealth Games in the Men's Road Race and is a rider who loves the cobbled classics - in particular, Ghent-Wevelgem, in which he finished fourth in 2009, and the 'Hell of the North', Paris-Roubaix.

Michael Rogers claimed overall victories in 2010 at the Tour of California and the Tour of Andalucia and is a three-time World Time Trial Champion - 2005, 2004, 2003.

THE TEAM: DID YOU KNOW?

Britain's professional road cycling team, Team Sky made their UCI ProTour debut and first appearance at the 2010 Santos Tour Down Under.

Team Sky's goal is to create the first British winner of the Tour de France within five years.
